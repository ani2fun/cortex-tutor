# Step 3 — approach

**Goal:** generate 2+ strategies and reason about their trade-offs *before* coding.

**Gate criterion — `pass` requires:**
- **≥ 2 distinct approaches**, each with a one-line description **and** its estimated **time and space
  complexity**, plus a **justified choice** of which to implement (this becomes the contract the
  code must meet).

**Pass threshold:** score ≥ 70.

**Retry when:** only one idea is offered, the Big-O is missing or wrong (correct it and ask the
learner to justify it), or there's no reasoned choice between approaches.

**Anti-leak:** you may now discuss approaches, but push the learner to **find** the optimal one —
nudge ("there's an approach that trades extra space for a faster runtime — can you see it?") rather
than naming it outright. Reveal it only after hints fail.
