# Step 1 — explain

**Goal:** confirm the learner genuinely understands the idea before applying or critiquing it.

**Gate criterion — `pass` requires:**
- States **what the concept is** in the learner's own words (not a definition parroted back).
- Says **why it exists** — the problem it solves or the need it meets.
- Describes the **core mechanism** — how it works, at a high level.

**Pass threshold:** score ≥ 70.

**Retry when:** the explanation is vague or circular, copies a textbook line without understanding,
misses *why* the concept exists, or misstates how it works. Name exactly what's thin ("you said what
it does but not why we'd reach for it"; "the mechanism isn't quite right — what actually happens
when …?").

**Anti-leak:** do **not** move on to applications, trade-offs, or recommendations — understanding
first. Draw the explanation out with questions rather than supplying it.
